Donkey has been left alone by Shrek and Fiona at the swamp and is equally thirsty and hungry. He then goes to Shrek’s house and sees two locked chests placed exactly the same distance from him. One is a beautiful golden chest with precious stones around it and a dragon-shaped golden ornament on the left that says “Only drinks,” and the other, on the right, is silver-colored with ancient ornaments and a dragon-shaped key inserted into it that says “Only food.”

Shrek had told Fiona to use a spell on Donkey before leaving the swamp to make him extremely assertive while Shrek is away, because Donkey doubts and hesitates a lot and usually takes too much time to make decisions. However, Fiona used a spell of rationality without telling Shrek.

Shrek explicitly told Donkey that there are drinks and food at the house but that he can only take from one chest; otherwise, he will be cursed by a magic spell.

Does Donkey choose the golden chest or the silver chest?
